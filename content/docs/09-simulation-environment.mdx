The Storm simulation lets the Marine Operations Manager rehearse a weather change, principally wind, and read how it would hit the reef and outdoor activity. The shift it gives the operator: from finding out conditions are unsafe once guests or the reef are already affected, to rehearsing a wind change and seeing the effect before it lands. In the Marine Operations view, scoped to North Reef here, Simulation sits alongside Reef Monitoring.

The run follows the same rhythm:

## Before running

![Storm simulation, before running. The left panel sets the wind scenario and the zones, with the forecasted conditions on the right.](07-simulation-storm-before.jpg)

| Control | What it sets |
| --- | --- |
| Forecast type | Wind on Dive, Wind on Mooring, or Combined |
| Wind condition | Calm (under 6 kn), Moderate (10-14 kn), High Wind (22-30 kn, advisory), or Storm (35 kn+, suspend) |
| Wind speed | Projected gust, here 14 kn |
| Zones included | Gaia Reef North, Gaia Reef South, Coral Garden E, House Reef |

### Creating an event

Under Event to simulate, New Event opens a builder for a condition the presets do not cover. You name the event and set its effect, then it joins the event list and can be selected into any run alongside the built-in events. This lets the environment persona model a scenario specific to the day rather than only the standard ones.

### Creating an action plan

Under Action plan, View All lists the saved plans and New Plan creates one. A plan is a named set of actions, with the action count shown beside it. Once saved, a custom plan is selectable and forecasts against any event the same way as the built-in plans, so the environment persona can prepare a tailored response and test it before committing.

## Reading the wind forecast

Read wind against the dive threshold first, then turbidity and air, since those decide whether activity continues.

| Metric | What it measures | Watch when |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Wind at reef | Wind over the dive zones | Crosses the dive threshold of 20 kn |
| Wind at bridge | Wind at the crossing | Rises against now, here +6 |
| Water turbidity | Sediment stirred into the water | Climbs in the beach zone, here +18% |
| AQI shift | Air quality change, often dust | Moves up, here +6 |

| Wind band | Wind | Posture |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Calm | under 6 kn | Settled |
| Moderate | 10 to 14 kn | Breeze |
| High Wind | 22 to 30 kn | Advisory |
| Storm | 35 kn and up | Suspend |

<Callout type="warning">
While simulation is on, the alert tray shows scenario alerts, not live ones. Live incidents are held until you close simulation.
</Callout>

## Implementing a plan

Once a run is forecasted, the right panel turns the result into action. It lists the plans the environment persona prepared for the event, each with its forecasted effect and an Implement control. Selecting Implement enacts that plan's actions: the dispatch goes to the marine and field teams, the action moves into the live operations view, and the forecast baseline re-anchors to track the plan as it runs. This is the step that takes a run from a what-if to an executed response.

A plan can be implemented as forecasted, or adjusted first by reopening it under Action plan, editing its actions, and re-running to confirm the new effect before dispatch.
