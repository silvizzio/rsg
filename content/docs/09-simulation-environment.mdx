The Storm simulation lets the Marine Operations Manager rehearse a weather change, principally wind, and read how it would hit the reef and outdoor activity. The shift it gives the operator: from finding out conditions are unsafe once guests or the reef are already affected, to rehearsing a wind change and seeing the effect before it lands. In the Marine Operations view, scoped to North Reef here, Simulation sits alongside Reef Monitoring.

Every run follows the same arc: you set the forecast conditions, the model forecasts the effect on the reef and the marine assets, and it proposes the rests, holds, and reroutes that protect both guests and the coral. The difference from Crowd and Traffic is the constraint, here the limits are environmental and safety thresholds, dive wind limits, water temperature, turbidity, and capacity at the reef, and the run is read against those thresholds.

## Before running

![Storm simulation, before running. The left panel sets the wind scenario and the zones, with the forecasted conditions on the right.](07-simulation-storm-before.jpg)

Before running sets the forecast conditions and the area in scope. Forecast type chooses what the wind is tested against, because the same wind matters differently to a dive boat than to a moored vessel. Wind condition is the trigger band and Wind speed the forecasted gust within it. Push the band up or add zones and the forecast tightens, crossing dive thresholds, lifting turbidity, and pulling more sites toward their limits, which is how the team tests a deteriorating forecast before committing the day's dive and marine schedule.

| Control | What it sets |
| --- | --- |
| Forecast type | Wind on Dive, Wind on Mooring, or Combined |
| Wind condition | Calm (under 6 kn), Moderate (10-14 kn), High Wind (22-30 kn, advisory), or Storm (35 kn+, suspend) |
| Wind speed | Forecasted gust, here 14 kn |
| Zones included | Gaia Reef North, Gaia Reef South, Coral Garden E, House Reef |

## After running

![Storm simulation, after running. A wind field sweeps the map and the right panel reads the forecasted outcome, the conditions, and the recommended actions.](07-simulation-storm-after.jpg)

The map fills with a wind field streaming across the water, so the exposed stretches read as places rather than numbers.

Forecasted outcome: a Wind Advisory with a one-line read on whether marine and outdoor activity is affected.

| Condition | Reading | Note |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Wind at bridge | 2.4 kn | +6 vs now |
| Wind at reef | 21 kn | dive threshold 20 |
| AQI shift | 48 | +6, dust |
| Water turbidity | +18% | beach zone |

The chain here is condition to threshold to call: the forecast pushes wind at the reef to the dive line and lifts turbidity, and the outcome banner translates that into whether the day's activities hold. Read each value against its named limit rather than on its own, since 21 kn at the reef matters because the dive threshold is 20.

| Trigger | Recommended action | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Temp rising | Rest Site B, shift afternoon groups to Site C | Water temp 29.8C trending toward the 30.5 threshold at Site B; resting it protects coral recovery |
| Capacity watch | Hold new arrivals at North Reef, route to Site B | 11 of 12 boats and 17 of 18 moorings in use; holding keeps North Reef within capacity |
| Over target | Follow up on low-rated arrival, review Marina to villas leg | Service score slipping on that leg |

## Reading the wind forecast

Read wind against the dive threshold first, then turbidity and air, since those decide whether activity continues.

| Metric | What it measures | Watch when |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Wind at reef | Wind over the dive zones | Crosses the dive threshold of 20 kn |
| Wind at bridge | Wind at the crossing | Rises against now, here +6 |
| Water turbidity | Sediment stirred into the water | Climbs in the beach zone, here +18% |
| AQI shift | Air quality change, often dust | Moves up, here +6 |

| Wind band | Wind | Posture |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Calm | under 6 kn | Settled |
| Moderate | 10 to 14 kn | Breeze |
| High Wind | 22 to 30 kn | Advisory |
| Storm | 35 kn and up | Suspend |

Two habits make the read reliable:

- Separate safety from comfort. Wind and dive limits are safety and they suspend activity; AQI and turbidity are mostly experience and reef health, they degrade the day and stress the coral without necessarily stopping operations.
- Act ahead of the threshold, not on it. The recommended actions rest a site as it trends toward a limit, water temp 29.8C toward a 30.5 threshold, because protecting coral recovery is cheaper before a breach than after. Watch capacity alongside conditions too: 11 of 12 boats and 17 of 18 moorings in use is a capacity call even on a calm forecast.

## Implementing a plan

Once a run is forecasted, the right panel turns the result into action. It lists the plans the environment persona prepared for the event, each with its forecasted effect and an Implement control. Selecting Implement enacts that plan's actions: the dispatch goes to the marine and field teams, the action moves into the live operations view, and the forecast baseline re-anchors to track the plan as it runs. This is the step that takes a run from a what-if to an executed response.

A plan can be implemented as forecasted, or adjusted first by reopening it under Action plan, editing its actions, and re-running to confirm the new effect before dispatch.

## Creating an event

Under Event to simulate, New Event opens a builder for a condition the presets do not cover. You name the event and set its effect, then it joins the event list and can be selected into any run alongside the built-in events. This lets the environment persona model a known incoming weather pattern or a site-specific event, a storm front, a sandstorm raising AQI and turbidity, a localized swell, rather than only the standard wind bands.

## Creating an action plan

Under Action plan, View All lists the saved plans and New Plan creates one. A plan is a named set of actions, with the action count shown beside it. Once saved, a custom plan is selectable and forecasts against any event the same way as the built-in plans, so the environment persona can prepare a standard response to a recurring threat, the high-wind dive protocol, the heat-stress reef rest, and confirm its forecasted effect before a deteriorating day arrives.
