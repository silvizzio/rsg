The Storm simulation is the marine and environment team's what-if tool for conditions. It takes a wind and weather forecast and a set of reef zones and forecasts whether dive, mooring, and outdoor activity stay safe and within capacity, then what to adjust. The shift it gives the operator: from finding out conditions are unsafe once guests or the reef are already affected, to rehearsing a wind change and seeing the effect before it lands.

The arc is the same trigger then effect then response: you set the forecast conditions, the model forecasts the effect on the reef and the marine assets, and it proposes the rests, holds, and reroutes that protect both guests and the coral. The difference from Crowd and Traffic is the constraint, the limits here are environmental and safety thresholds, dive wind limits, water temperature, turbidity, and capacity at the reef, not fleet or queue, and the run is read against those thresholds.

In the Marine Operations view, scoped to North Reef here, Simulation sits alongside Reef Monitoring, and the run follows the same rhythm:

## Before running

![Storm simulation, before running. The left panel sets the wind scenario and the zones, with the forecasted conditions on the right.](07-simulation-storm-before.jpg)

Before running sets the forecast conditions and the area in scope. Analysis Window (Now, Daily, Weekly) sets the horizon. Forecast Type chooses what the wind is tested against: Wind on Dive, Wind on Mooring, or Combined, because the same wind matters differently to a dive boat than to a moored vessel.

Wind Condition is the trigger band: Calm (under 6 kn, settled), Moderate (10 to 14 kn, breeze), High Wind (22 to 30 kn, advisory), or Storm (35 kn and up, suspend). Wind Speed sets the forecasted gust within that band (14 kn here). Zones Included (Gaia Reef North, Gaia Reef South, Coral Garden E, House Reef) scopes which reefs the forecast covers.

Push the wind band up or add zones and the forecast tightens, crossing dive thresholds, raising turbidity, pulling more sites toward their limits, which is how the team tests a deteriorating forecast before committing the day's dive and marine schedule.

| Control | What it sets |
| --- | --- |
| Forecast type | Wind on Dive, Wind on Mooring, or Combined |
| Wind condition | Calm (under 6 kn), Moderate (10-14 kn), High Wind (22-30 kn, advisory), or Storm (35 kn+, suspend) |
| Wind speed | Forecasted gust, here 14 kn |
| Zones included | Gaia Reef North, Gaia Reef South, Coral Garden E, House Reef |

## After running

![Storm simulation, after running. A wind field sweeps the map and the right panel reads the forecasted outcome, the conditions, and the recommended actions.](07-simulation-storm-after.jpg)

After running, the right panel forecasts conditions against the safety and environmental thresholds. The Forecasted Outcome banner is the activity call, here Wind Advisory: calm conditions, all marine and outdoor activity unaffected. In a heavier run this is where a suspend or advisory would be stated.

Forecasted Conditions carries the detail, each value read against its limit. Wind at bridge 2.4 kn (+6 vs now); Wind at reef 21 kn against a dive threshold of 20, so the reef wind is the one sitting on its limit. AQI shift 48 (+6, dust) and Water turbidity +18% (beach zone) are the environmental movers, dust in the air and sediment in the water, both of which affect the guest experience and the reef even when wind is within limits.

A wind field also sweeps the map, so the exposed stretches read as places rather than numbers. The chain here is condition to threshold to call: the forecast pushes reef wind to the dive line and lifts turbidity, and the outcome banner translates that into whether the day's activities hold.

## Reading the wind forecast

Reading the forecast is comparing each condition to its threshold and protecting the reef ahead of a breach, not after. Read wind against the dive threshold first, then turbidity and air, since those decide whether activity continues. The reading rules:

- Read each value against its named limit. Wind at reef 21 kn means little alone; against a dive threshold of 20 it means dives at that site are over the line. The threshold, not the number, is the decision.
- Separate safety from comfort. Wind and dive limits are safety, they suspend activity. AQI and turbidity are mostly experience and reef health, they degrade the day and stress the coral without necessarily stopping operations.
- Act ahead of the threshold, not on it. The recommended actions rest a site as it trends toward a limit, water temp 29.8C toward a 30.5 threshold, because protecting coral recovery is cheaper before a breach than after.
- Watch capacity alongside conditions. A reef can be within wind limits and still over capacity, 11 of 12 boats and 17 of 18 moorings in use is a capacity call even on a calm forecast.

| Metric | What it measures | Watch when |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Wind at reef | Wind over the dive zones | Crosses the dive threshold of 20 kn |
| Wind at bridge | Wind at the crossing | Rises against now, here +6 |
| Water turbidity | Sediment stirred into the water | Climbs in the beach zone, here +18% |
| AQI shift | Air quality change, often dust | Moves up, here +6 |

| Wind band | Wind | Posture |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Calm | under 6 kn | Settled |
| Moderate | 10 to 14 kn | Breeze |
| High Wind | 22 to 30 kn | Advisory |
| Storm | 35 kn and up | Suspend |

## Creating an event

Creating an event authors a condition the bands do not cover. New Event opens a builder to define and name a custom condition, a specific storm front, a sandstorm raising AQI and turbidity, a localized swell, and set its effect. Once saved it runs like any preset. This lets the environment persona model a known incoming weather pattern or a site-specific event rather than only the standard wind bands.
