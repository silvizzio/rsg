The Environment Manager view is about keeping air, wind, and water conditions safe for guests, and catching anything unsafe before guests are affected. It shows live conditions across the resort and, just as important, it watches the cameras for violations such as swimming in a restricted area and alerts security the moment one is detected, instead of after the fact.

![The wide view of the destination, with the environmental read along the bottom: air quality, wind, and water.](05-env-lod1-1.jpg)

## What you see

| Field | Example | What it tells you |
|---|---|---|
| Air quality | 44, Good | Air quality index for the destination |
| Dust | PM2.5 14, PM10 +32 | Fine and coarse particulate readings |
| Wind | 12.4 kn NW, Beaufort 4 | Speed and direction, with gusts |
| Water quality | 7.4 of 10 | Overall water quality index |
| Clarity | Light cloud, turbidity 4.2 | How clear the water is |
| Surface temperature | 27.1 C | Sea surface temperature at the beach |

## The zone view

At the district level the view sits over one zone, here Shura Marina, with the environmental strip along the bottom. Before, unsafe conditions were noticed once guests were already affected. Here, air, wind, and water are visible live, so safety calls come first.

## The close-up: catching a violation

Before, unauthorized access was discovered after the fact. Here, the cameras detect it and alert security in real time.

![Close-up of Turtle Bay. The red card calls security to SLS Red Sea for unauthorized swimmers, because 2 people are in a restricted reef zone, the alert is 1 minute old, and water quality reads 94.](05-env-lod3-safety-violation-detection.jpg)

## Working in this view

1. Open the Environment Manager from the persona bar.
2. Read the destination air, wind, and water bands at the wide view.
3. Select a zone to inspect its environmental detail.
4. Open a violation alert to see the affected site and the recommended action.
