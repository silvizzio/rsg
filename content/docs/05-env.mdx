The Environment Manager view exists to keep air, wind, and water conditions safe for guests, and to catch anything unsafe before guests are affected by it. It does this in two ways at once. It shows live environmental conditions across the resort, and, just as importantly, it watches the camera feeds for violations such as swimming in a restricted area, alerting security the moment one is detected rather than after the fact.

![The wide view of the destination, with the environmental read along the bottom: air quality, wind, and water.](05-env-lod1-1.jpg)

## What you see

At the wide view, the bottom strip is the environmental read for the whole destination, covering air quality, wind, and water.

| Field | Example | What it tells you |
|---|---|---|
| Air quality | 44, Good | Air quality index for the destination |
| Dust | PM2.5 14, PM10 +32 | Fine and coarse particulate readings |
| Wind | 12.4 kn NW, Beaufort 4 | Speed and direction, with gusts |
| Water quality | 7.4 of 10 | Overall water quality index |
| Clarity | Light cloud, turbidity 4.2 | How clear the water is |
| Surface temperature | 27.1 C | Sea surface temperature at the beach |

## The zone view

At the district level, the view focuses on a single zone, in this example Shura Marina, with the environmental monitoring strip displayed along the bottom of the screen.

Previously, unsafe conditions were often only identified after guests had already been affected, or after an incident had begun to escalate. With this view, the key environmental factors, air quality, wind conditions, and water status, are monitored and displayed in real time.

This gives the team earlier visibility of potential risks and allows safety-related decisions to be made proactively, before guest experience or operations are impacted. In short, the zone view shifts the response from reacting to issues after they occur to making informed safety calls ahead of time.

## The close-up

At the closest level the view drills into a single site for the two things that keep the resort safe: the monitoring staying live, and violations being caught the moment they happen.

### Keeping monitoring live

Monitoring is only useful for as long as the sensors are actually reporting. Here, a probe dropping offline or a camera degrading is caught at once, with a clear instruction to restore coverage before a blind spot can open up.

![Close-up of Shura Marina. The red card sends a technician to Lagoon East because water probe LG-04 has been offline for 6 minutes and reef camera RB-01 is degraded, leaving 41 of 43 monitoring assets reporting.](05-env-lod3-monitoring-assets.jpg)

### Catching a violation

Previously, unauthorized access to a restricted area was discovered after the fact, if it was discovered at all. Here, the cameras detect it and alert security in real time, turning a delayed report into an immediate response.

![Close-up of Turtle Bay. The red card calls security to SLS Red Sea for unauthorized swimmers, because 2 people are in a restricted reef zone, the alert is 1 minute old, and water quality reads 94.](05-env-lod3-safety-violation-detection.jpg)

## Working in this view

1. Open the Environment Manager from the persona bar.
2. Read the destination air, wind, and water bands at the wide view.
3. Select a zone to inspect its environmental detail.
4. Open a violation alert to see the affected site and the recommended action.
