The Guest Experience Manager view is about spotting guest mobility problems before a guest ever feels them. Usually a station looks fine right up until people are queuing and complaining. This view shows demand building so the manager can act before anyone waits too long. It also gives a live picture of every guest on the move and what they are travelling in, and it ties a low rating back to the exact trip that caused it. This is a read and oversight view; the actual dispatching happens in the Operations Manager view.

![The wide view across Red Sea Global. The strip along the bottom is the guest mobility read for the whole destination.](03-gxm-lod1-1.jpg)

## What you see

| Field | Example | What it tells you |
|---|---|---|
| Guest travel satisfaction | 4.4 of 5 | How guests are rating their transfers right now |
| Guests travelling | 42 | Guests on the move from the focused station |
| Transfer variance | +42% | How far transfer demand is running above or below plan |
| Booking requests | 86 | Open mobility requests, flagged when over capacity |
| Available vehicles | 6 of 12 | Vehicles free to assign |
| Vehicle mix | 58% buggy, 32% shuttle | What guests are riding in |
| Stations over capacity | 3 of 5 | Stations running beyond their limit |
| Average trip | 6.8 min, 1.8 km | Typical travel time and distance |

## The district view

At the district level the view holds one district, here the Marina, with the guest mobility strip along the bottom.

## Following a guest trip

At the closest level the view walks one guest trip through three phases, and each phase surfaces its own recommended action.

### Before the trip

Before, an overwhelmed station only showed up once guests complained. Here, demand is visible early enough to act.

![Close-up of the Marina, before the trip. The red card asks the Operations team for 6 more transfer boats, because demand has reached 214 against a capacity of 150, only 11 vehicles are free, and the oldest request has waited 8 minutes.](03-gxm-lod3-pre-trip.jpg)

### During the trip

Before, there was no clear picture of how many guests were moving. Here, every guest in motion is visible, along with what they are riding in.

![Close-up of the Marina, during the trip. The card suggests sending 2 buggies to the Spine Corridor, because 318 guests are on the move and the lead buggy is already at 5 of 6 seats.](03-gxm-lod3-trip.jpg)

### After the trip

Before, satisfaction scores arrived with no link to a cause. Here, a low score points straight back to the leg that caused it.

![Close-up of the Marina, after the trip. The card flags a low-rated arrival and suggests reviewing the Marina to villas leg, because the rating is averaging 4.1 of 5 with one group at 3.6 and travel time of 8.4 minutes against a 6.3 minute target.](03-gxm-lod3-after-trip.jpg)

## Working in this view

1. Open the Guest Experience Manager from the persona bar.
2. Read the destination mobility strip at the wide view.
3. Select a district to inspect its stations and demand.
4. Open a station to follow its guest trip and read the recommended action.

<Callout type="note">
This is a read and oversight view. Dispatch and incident actions are carried out in the Operations Manager view.
</Callout>
