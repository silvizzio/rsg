The Guest Experience Manager view exists to catch guest mobility problems before a guest ever feels one. In day-to-day operation a station can look perfectly healthy right up until the moment people are queuing and the complaints begin, and by then the problem is already a guest experience problem. This view is built to surface demand as it builds, so the manager can act while there is still time to keep anyone from waiting too long.

It does three things in particular. It shows demand rising before it turns into a queue. It gives a live picture of every guest on the move and the vehicle they are travelling in. And it ties a low satisfaction rating back to the specific trip that caused it, so a soft number finally has a cause attached. This is a read and oversight view: the manager watches, judges, and recommends here, while the actual dispatching is carried out in the Operations Manager view.

## What you see

At the wide view, the strip along the bottom is the guest mobility read for the whole destination. It is organised into the three phases of the guest journey, read left to right: what is building before a trip, what is moving during it, and what the trip left behind. Two station lists sit alongside, breaking the same picture down by where the demand is concentrating.

**Pre-trip** is the supply and demand picture before guests move.

| Field | Example | What it tells you |
|---|---|---|
| Available vehicles | 6 of 12 | Vehicles free to assign now, and how many are Marina-assigned |
| Transfer variance | +18% | How far transfer demand is running above or below the planned 240 per hour |
| Booking requests | 412 | Mobility requests received in the last 60 minutes |

**Trip** is who is moving right now and in what.

| Field | Example | What it tells you |
|---|---|---|
| Vehicle type mix | 22 buggy, 9 shuttle, 5 bus, 4 tender | The active fleet broken down by vehicle class |
| Guest per vehicle type | 22 buggy, 9 shuttle, 5 bus, 4 tender | How guests on the move split across those same classes |
| Guests travelling | 186 | Guests in transit right now |

**After-trip** is the outcome once guests have arrived.

| Field | Example | What it tells you |
|---|---|---|
| Avg travel distance | 2.4 km | Typical distance covered per trip today |
| Avg travel time | 8.4 min | Typical door-to-door time today |
| Guest travel satisfaction | 4.6 of 5 | How guests are rating transfers, across 920 trips today, with the change on last week |

Two station lists sit to the left of the strip. Request time by station ranks where requests are landing, here Marina Arrival, SLS Lobby, and EDITION Beach Club. Top stations ranks the busiest pickup points by request volume, here Marina, Turtle Bay Arrival, and Spine Corridor. Together they point to the part of the destination driving the destination-wide numbers.

## The district view

At the district level the view narrows to a single district, here the Marina, and the guest mobility strip rescopes with it. The same three phases now describe one district rather than the whole destination, which is usually where a destination-wide signal resolves into the place that is driving it.

![The Marina district. The strip now reads for the Marina alone: 4 of 12 vehicles free, transfer variance of +42% against plan, 86 booking requests in the last hour, 32 guests on the move, and a 4.6 of 5 travel rating across 240 trips today. The station lists confirm the Marina is driving its own demand, with Marina Arrival, Boardwalk, and Boat Slip taking the top requests.](03-gxm-lod2.jpg)

## Following a guest trip

At the closest level the view follows a single guest trip through its three phases, and each phase surfaces its own recommended action. Read together, the three phases trace the journey from the demand that precedes a trip, through the trip itself, to the satisfaction it leaves behind.

### Before the trip

Previously, an overwhelmed station only became visible once guests were already complaining. Here, demand is visible early enough to do something about it, so extra capacity can be requested before the queue ever forms.

![Close-up of the Marina, before the trip. The red card asks the Operations team for 6 more transfer boats, because demand has reached 214 against a capacity of 150, only 11 vehicles are free, and the oldest request has waited 8 minutes.](03-gxm-lod3-pre-trip.jpg)

### During the trip

Previously, there was no clear picture of how many guests were actually moving at any moment. Here, every guest in motion is visible, along with the vehicle they are travelling in, so a developing pinch point can be relieved while the trip is still under way.

![Close-up of the Marina, during the trip. The card suggests sending 2 buggies to the Spine Corridor, because 318 guests are on the move and the lead buggy is already at 5 of 6 seats.](03-gxm-lod3-trip.jpg)

### After the trip

Previously, satisfaction scores arrived after the fact with no link to a cause. Here, a low score points straight back to the leg that produced it, so the manager can trace dissatisfaction to a specific operational pattern rather than guessing at it.

![Close-up of the Marina, after the trip. The card flags a low-rated arrival and suggests reviewing the Marina to villas leg, because the rating is averaging 4.1 of 5 with one group at 3.6 and travel time of 8.4 minutes against a 6.3 minute target.](03-gxm-lod3-after-trip.jpg)

## Working in this view

1. Open the Guest Experience Manager from the persona bar.
2. Read the destination mobility strip at the wide view.
3. Select a district to inspect its stations and demand.
4. Open a station to follow its guest trip and read the recommended action.

<Callout type="note">
This is a read and oversight view. Dispatch and incident actions are carried out in the Operations Manager view.
</Callout>
