The IOC is a single 3D map wrapped in a fixed frame of regions: a header across the top, a metrics panel on the left, a summary strip along the bottom, a legend and a recommended-action card over the map, a camera panel on the right, and a tool strip on the right edge. The frame stays the same for every persona and every destination. Only the content inside each region changes. The examples below show one persona, but each region works the same way for all four.

## The header

The header runs across the top and does not change. On the left it names the operations centre and the destination in focus, here Shura Island. The bar in the middle is a compass and orientation readout for the 3D view. To its right is a live environment readout, wind, sea, air, and air quality, which is destination-wide and the same whatever persona you are in. On the right sit the account menu and the clock. Your login is tied to a single persona, so everything you see belongs to that persona, and the menu shows who you are signed in as.

![The header. Destination identity on the left, the live wind, sea, air, and air-quality readout in the centre right, and the account menu and clock on the right.](02-interface-header.jpg)

## The left panel

The left panel holds the active persona's numbers for whatever place is in focus. The top names the persona and the place, here Guest Experience at the Marina. Below it an analysis window switches the reading between Now, Daily, and Weekly. The rest is a stack of metric cards grouped into labelled sections. The sections and cards are whatever that persona tracks. The example here splits the guest trip into Pre-Trip, Trip, and After-Trip, but the layout, a window toggle above grouped cards, is the same in every persona.

## The bottom strip

The strip along the bottom is a quick read of the headline numbers, grouped by theme. It rescopes with the map detail level. At the wide view it reads the whole destination.

![The bottom strip at the wide view, reading the whole destination.](02-interface-bottom-lod1.jpg)

Drill into a district and the same strip rescopes to that district. The title picks up the district name and the fields shift to district-scoped versions, such as stations over capacity for that district. Same persona, narrower scope.

![The same strip at the district view, rescoped to one district, with the district name added to the title.](02-interface-bottom-lod2.jpg)

## The map legend

The legend sits at the bottom left of the map. The severity key colour-codes items from Critical down to Low and Info, with a live count beside each, so you can see at a glance how many things need attention and how serious they are. Below it, the data inputs line names the feed driving the current view and shows whether it is live. Collapse the legend with the chevron when you need the map space.

## The recommended action card

This is what makes the IOC proactive. When the system spots something, it raises a card over the map: a badge naming the trigger state, here Temp Rising, a plain recommended action as the headline, a live camera of the place, a one-line reason with the numbers behind it, and two buttons. The primary button carries the action out, Remind me later defers it, and the X dismisses it. The card pattern is identical in every persona, only the trigger and the action change. These cards appear at the close-up level when a threshold is crossed.

## The CCTV panel

The right side is the camera wall for the area in focus. The header carries two viewing modes, Auto Roaming and Virtual Patrol, a breadcrumb of where you are, a search box, a grid-size selector, and the camera count. Below are the live tiles, each labelled with its name and ID. Page through with the controls at the bottom, change the grid size to show more or fewer at once, search for a specific camera, and open any tile full-screen for a closer look. Selecting a tile also focuses its location on the map. The panel appears wherever cameras are available for the persona.

## The toolbar

A slim tool strip runs down the right edge in two groups. The top group holds the weather overlay, the history and time control, and simulation. The bottom group holds search and the zoom controls. Simulation opens from the top group and has its own section. Everything here is the same for every persona.

## Personas and your login

The IOC has four persona views: Guest Experience Manager, Operations Manager, Environment Manager, and Marine Operations Manager, each covered in its own chapter. Your login is tied to one of them. When you sign in, the whole interface, the left panel, bottom strip, legend feed, and cameras, loads for your persona, and that is the view you work in. Each persona is a separate signed-in experience, so you only ever see your own.

## Navigating map detail

1. Start at the wide view for the whole destination.
2. Select a district to drop to the district view, and watch the bottom strip rescope.
3. Select a site to drop to the close-up view, where the recommended-action cards appear.
4. Zoom back out with the toolbar, or step up one level at a time.

<Callout type="tip">
The frame is constant but everything inside it is persona-scoped. The numbers, cameras, and alerts you see always belong to your persona, which is set by your login.
</Callout>
