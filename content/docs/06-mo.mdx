The Marine Operations Manager view exists to protect the coral reef, Red Sea Global's most valuable natural asset. Reef damage tends to happen quietly, and by the time it is visible it is usually permanent, so the only effective protection is early warning. This view provides that warning in three ways. It gives live sight of reef turbidity and temperature. It shows how many boats and divers are near the reef at any moment, so the site never becomes overcrowded. And when a threshold is crossed, it raises the alarm and assigns the response automatically, rather than leaving it to manual spotting and coordination.

![The wide view of the destination waters. The bottom strip is the marine read: reef conditions, mooring, dive operation, and wind.](06-mo-lod1-1.jpg)

## What you see

At the wide view, the bottom strip is the marine read for the destination waters, covering reef conditions, mooring, dive operation, and wind.

| Field | Example | What it tells you |
|---|---|---|
| Turbidity | 2.4, clear | Reef water cloudiness against a 5.0 threshold |
| Reef temperature | 26.4 C | Sea temperature, with a bleach-risk flag above threshold |
| Mooring | 18 of 24 used | Moorings in use and free |
| Dive sites | 3 of 4 open | Sites open, with any closed |
| Divers in water | 12 | Divers down across active sites |
| Visibility | 18 m | Underwater visibility, tied to turbidity |
| Wind on marine | 15.2 kn NW | Wind over the water, with a 20 kn dive-closure trigger |

## The marine zone view

At the district level the view sits over the dive and mooring water. Previously, there was no reliable count of how many boats and divers were near the reef. Here, capacity is visible in real time, so crowding can be prevented before it does any physical harm to the reef.

![The Shura Marina dive and mooring water. Mooring is full at 6 of 6 with a divert recommended, 4 divers are down in 2 groups, and visibility is 22 metres.](06-mo-lod2-1.jpg)

![The same water, wider, looking across the bay to the causeway.](06-mo-lod2-2.jpg)

## The close-up: active response

Previously, dealing with a problem meant spotting it manually and then coordinating a response by hand. Here, when a threshold is crossed the system raises the alarm and assigns the task automatically, so the response is already moving by the time an operator looks.

![Close-up of North Reef. The card diverts dive groups from North Reef to Site C, because turbidity has reached 5.2 against a 5.0 threshold with 11 boats at the reef, and the task is auto-assigned to marine security.](06-mo-lod3-active-response.jpg)

## Working in this view

1. Open the Marine Operations Manager from the persona bar.
2. Read reef conditions, mooring, and dive operation across the waters at the wide view.
3. Select a marine zone to inspect dive and mooring capacity.
4. Open a reef site to see the threshold breach and the automatic response.
