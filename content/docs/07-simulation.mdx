Simulation lets operators rehearse a what-if on the twin without touching live data. You raise or change one condition, then watch how the destination responds: how crowds and traffic move, where mobility or the environment comes under strain, and what the system would recommend. The same recommended-action cards that appear in live operation appear here, so a scenario can be walked through from start to finish before it ever happens.

Simulation opens from the top button on the right toolbar. The button is in place; the simulation screens are being exported and will be added here.

## What you can simulate

### Guest numbers

Raise the number of guests and watch the effect on mobility: the health of the transfer network, how many buses are needed, and the load on the EV chargers. The view returns sample recommended actions, such as adding vehicles or moving them to where demand is building.

<ScreenshotPlaceholder caption="Guest-number simulation with recommended actions" />

### Weather

Raise or lower the wind speed and watch the effect on the environment, with recommended actions for the new conditions.

<ScreenshotPlaceholder caption="Wind-speed simulation with recommended actions" />

## Crowd and traffic on the map

While a simulation runs, crowd density and traffic flow are drawn straight onto the map, so you can see where people and vehicles build up as the scenario plays. Density reads as High, Medium, or Low, and a sustained High area is what raises a crowd density alert in live operation.

<ScreenshotPlaceholder caption="Crowd density and traffic flow during a simulation, Shura Island" />

## Running a simulation

1. Open simulation from the top button on the right toolbar.
2. Pick what to change: guest numbers or wind speed.
3. Adjust the level and let the scenario play.
4. Read the recommended actions the system returns.
5. Close simulation to return to live data.

<Callout type="warning">
While simulation is on, the alert tray shows scenario alerts, not live ones. Live incidents are held until you close simulation.
</Callout>
