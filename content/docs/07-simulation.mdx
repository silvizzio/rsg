Simulation lets operators rehearse a what-if on the twin without touching live data or live operations. You build a scenario, run it, read the forecast, and dispatch the recommended actions the run surfaces, the same recommended-action thinking that drives live operation. It lets a team walk a busy day through before it happens and decide which response holds the service level best.

The client scopes simulation as two what-if families, both ending in recommended actions:

- Mobility: a change in guest volume and its effect on movement, the fleet, charging, and the road network. Built across the Guest Experience and Operations views.
- Environmental: a change in weather, principally wind, and its effect on the reef and outdoor activity. Built in the Marine Operations view.

All three open from the top button on the right toolbar and follow the same rhythm:

1. Set up the scenario.
2. Run it.
3. Read the forecast.
4. Dispatch a recommended action, or compare to the baseline.
5. Exit to return to live data.

Every run closes with recommended-action cards. Each carries a trigger badge naming the condition, a plain-language action as the headline, the numbers behind it, and a dispatch control, the same shape as the live recommended-action card so a forecast leads straight to a decision.

## Guest Experience simulation: the crowd

Answers where guests build up across the destination. The shift: from a Guest Experience Manager who finds out a station is overwhelmed when guests complain, to one who sees demand building and acts before anyone waits too long.

### Before running

![Guest Experience simulation, before running. The left rail builds the scenario, the right rail shows the live baseline, and Run Simulation starts it.](07-simulation-before.jpg)

| Control | What it sets |
| --- | --- |
| Phase | Pre-Trip, Trip, or Post-Trip |
| Event to simulate | Peak Arrival Surge (+180% transfer wave at Marina), VIP Convoy (reserved routing), Weather Disruption (tender hold, wind 24 kn), or a New Event |
| Guest volume | Guests expected on the island, here 2,500 |
| Intensity preset | Normal, Peak, Surge, or VIP |
| Pre-trip variables | Marina capacity 32 per hour, buffer time 6 minutes |
| Response plan | Baseline (do nothing), Recall and Relieve (3 actions), or Reroute and Advise (2 actions) |

### Reading the crowd forecast

Read it in three moves: the heatmap shows where pressure builds, the mobility health index shows how bad, and the forecasted KPIs show why.

| Metric | What it measures | Watch when |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Mobility health index | Whole-network health, 0 to 100, healthy at 85 | Falls below 85; the lower it goes, the closer to capacity |
| Transfer variance | Gap between planned and actual transfers | Climbs off the baseline, here +18% to +34% |
| Booking requests | Demand on the mobility service | Rises faster than vehicles can clear, here 412 to 680 |
| Available vehicles | Free vehicles against the fleet | Falls toward zero, here 28 to 18 |
| Request age | How long the average request has waited | Rises past the service target |

Compare to Baseline holds the forecast against now; Exit Simulation returns to live data.

## Operations simulation: the fleet and traffic

Answers how the fleet and the road network hold up under the same pressure. The shift: from a manager who finds out something is wrong too late, to one who always knows what is running, what is at risk, and what is out of service. In the Operations Manager view, Simulation sits alongside Fleet, Utilization, Drivers, and EV.

| Forecast type | Vehicle, Charging, or Combined |

| Guest volume | Expected on-island, here 2,500 |
| Number of vehicles | Available on-island, here 800 |

### Reading the fleet forecast

Read the verdict first, then the metric that drove it: utilization and charging set capacity risk, fatigue and maintenance set continuity risk.

| Metric | What it measures | Watch when |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Utilization at peak | Share of the fleet in use at peak | Approaches 100%, here 96% |
| Idle time | Share of the fleet sitting unused | Very low means over-stretched, very high means waste |
| Charging demand | Load on the EV chargers | Chargers hit capacity, here 3 maxed at 84% |
| Energy needed | Extra energy the scenario draws | Rises against typical, here +620 kWh |
| Maintenance backlog | Vehicles waiting on service | Grows across the shift, here +2 by end of shift |
| Fatigue risk | Drivers near a fatigue limit | Any count above zero, here 3 on the bridge |

## Marine Operations simulation: wind and storm

Answers how wind and storm conditions hit the reef and outdoor activity. The shift: from a Marine Operations Manager who finds out conditions are unsafe once guests or the reef are already affected, to one who rehearses a wind change and sees the effect before it lands. In the Marine Operations view, Simulation sits alongside Reef Monitoring.

### Before running

![Marine Operations simulation, before running. The left rail sets the wind scenario and the zones, with the forecasted conditions on the right.](07-simulation-storm-before.jpg)

### Reading the wind forecast

Read wind against the dive threshold first, then turbidity and air, since those decide whether activity continues.

| Metric | What it measures | Watch when |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Wind at reef | Wind over the dive zones | Crosses the dive threshold of 20 kn |
| Wind at bridge | Wind at the crossing | Rises against now, here +6 |
| Water turbidity | Sediment stirred into the water | Climbs in the beach zone, here +18% |
| AQI shift | Air quality change, often dust | Moves up, here +6 |

| Wind band | Wind | Posture |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Calm | under 6 kn | Settled |
| Moderate | 10 to 14 kn | Breeze |
| High Wind | 22 to 30 kn | Advisory |
| Storm | 35 kn and up | Suspend |

## Running a simulation

1. Open simulation from the top button on the right toolbar.
2. Build the scenario for your view: events and response plan in Guest Experience, forecast type and fleet assumptions in Operations, or wind condition and zones in Marine Operations.
3. Choose an intensity, scenario, or wind preset.
4. Run the simulation.
5. Read the forecast: the crowd outlook, the fleet and traffic outlook, or the wind and reef outlook.
6. Dispatch a recommended action to its team, or use Compare to Baseline.
7. Exit Simulation to return to live data.

<Callout type="warning">
While simulation is on, the alert tray shows scenario alerts, not live ones. Live incidents are held until you close simulation.
</Callout>
