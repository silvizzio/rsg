The Operations Manager view exists to catch fleet problems before they ever reach a guest. Breakdowns, delays, and idle vehicles rarely announce themselves; left unwatched, they surface later as complaints, missed transfers, and rising cost. This view is built to give early sight of the fleet, so each of those problems can be acted on while it is still small.

It watches four things at once: what is running and what is at risk, whether the fleet is being used well, driver safety and compliance, and the state of every EV charger. The aim across all four is the same, to move from finding out something is wrong too late to always knowing what is running, what is under strain, and what needs attention before it turns into a cost or a service problem.

## The hub view

At the district level the view focuses on a single hub, here Turtle Bay, and the strip rescopes from the whole destination down to that hub. Reading a hub against the destination figure is what shows whether a problem is local to one hub or shared across the whole fleet.

![Zoomed to the Turtle Bay hub. The strip rescopes to that hub: 14 of 16 vehicles in service, hub use of 68% against the island 79%, and all 6 EV bays active.](04-ops-lod2-1.jpg)

## The close-up: four things to stay ahead of

At the closest level the view drills into a site and raises a recommended action for each of the four areas. Each card frames the same shift, from reacting after the fact to acting on an early signal.

### Fleet availability

Previously, you found out something was wrong too late to prevent it. Here, you always know what is running, what is at risk, and what is out of service, so capacity can be rebalanced before it bites.

![Close-up of Turtle Bay, fleet availability. The card suggests pooling reception trips to fill buggies, because 18 of 24 buggies are running at only 2 of 6 seats, so pooling frees about 6 vehicles.](04-ops-lod3-fleet-availability.jpg)

### Utilization and efficiency

Previously, there was no clear sense of whether the fleet was being used well. Here, overworked and idle assets show up early, so a vehicle sitting empty in one place can be moved to where demand is building.

![Close-up of Turtle Bay, utilization. The card suggests moving an idle pod to where demand is, because it is sitting empty while 4 single-rider sedans queue and 5 assets are under-used.](04-ops-lod3-utilization-efficiency.jpg)

### Safety and compliance

Previously, violations and incidents were reported after they had happened. Here, driver behaviour and compliance gaps show up before they escalate, so a marshal can be sent while the situation is still recoverable.

![Close-up of Turtle Bay, safety. The card suggests sending a safety marshal to the causeway queue, because a buggy is over capacity at 7 of 6 and helmet and tailgating flags are rising on the bridge.](04-ops-lod3-safety-compliance.jpg)

### EV charging

Previously, you learned a charger was down only when someone told you. Here, a live map shows what is working, what is not, and how much energy is left, so vehicles can be routed to the chargers that can actually serve them.

![Close-up of Turtle Bay, EV charging. The card suggests routing 2 EVs to the Turtle Bay charger after drop-off, because both are under 65% and the charger is 84% free.](04-ops-lod3-ev-infrastructure.jpg)

## Working in this view

1. Open the Operations Manager from the persona bar.
2. Read the destination operations strip at the wide view.
3. Select a hub to rescope the fleet, utilization, safety, and EV figures.
4. Open a site to read its recommended action and dispatch a response.
