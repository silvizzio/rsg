The Operations Manager view is about catching fleet problems before they reach a guest. Breakdowns, delays, and idle vehicles do not announce themselves; they usually surface as complaints. This view gives early sight of four things: what is running and what is at risk, whether the fleet is being used well, driver safety and compliance, and the state of every EV charger. The goal is to act on each one before it becomes a cost or a service problem.

![The wide view of the destination. The bottom strip is the operations read: fleet and maintenance, utilization, driver safety, and EV charging.](04-ops-lod1-1.jpg)

At the district level the view focuses on one hub, here Turtle Bay, and the strip rescopes from the whole destination to that hub.

## The close-up: four things to stay ahead of

At the closest level the view drills into a site and surfaces a recommended action for each of the four areas.

### Fleet availability

Before: you find out something is wrong too late. Here: you always know what is running, what is at risk, and what is out of service.

![Close-up of Turtle Bay, fleet availability. The card suggests pooling reception trips to fill buggies, because 18 of 24 buggies are running at only 2 of 6 seats, so pooling frees about 6 vehicles.](04-ops-lod3-fleet-availability.jpg)

### Utilization and efficiency

Before: no clear sense of whether the fleet is used well. Here: overworked and idle assets show up early.

![Close-up of Turtle Bay, utilization. The card suggests moving an idle pod to where demand is, because it is sitting empty while 4 single-rider sedans queue and 5 assets are under-used.](04-ops-lod3-utilization-efficiency.jpg)

### Safety and compliance

Before: violations and incidents are reported after the fact. Here: driver behaviour and compliance gaps show up before they escalate.

![Close-up of Turtle Bay, safety. The card suggests sending a safety marshal to the causeway queue, because a buggy is over capacity at 7 of 6 and helmet and tailgating flags are rising on the bridge.](04-ops-lod3-safety-compliance.jpg)

### EV charging

Before: you learn a charger is down when someone tells you. Here: a live map shows what is working, what is not, and how much energy is left.

![Close-up of Turtle Bay, EV charging. The card suggests routing 2 EVs to the Turtle Bay charger after drop-off, because both are under 65% and the charger is 84% free.](04-ops-lod3-ev-infrastructure.jpg)

## Working in this view

1. Open the Operations Manager from the persona bar.
2. Read the destination operations strip at the wide view.
3. Select a hub to rescope the fleet, utilization, safety, and EV figures.
4. Open a site to read its recommended action and dispatch a response.
